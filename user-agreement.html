1. What Ona is

Ona is a local, offline social feed simulation for iPhone. You create a profile and posts. Other participants in the feed are simulated characters generated by software and on-device artificial intelligence. They are not real people. They do not have real lives, real identities, or real awareness of you.

Ona also offers an optional Broadcast mode: you may turn on your front camera for a private, on-device live preview while simulated residents send short floating emote reactions. Broadcasts are not streamed to the internet and have no live comments.

The app may present these characters using social-media-style language such as “people” or “residents” for immersion. That wording is fictional framing only. It must not be understood as a statement that they are human.

  • Any “relationship,” “friendship,” or emotional bond you experience is one-sided and directed at a simulation.
  • Characters may say things that seem personal, intimate, or supportive. Those statements are generated text, not genuine human intent.

Ona is entertainment and creative simulation, not a substitute for human connection, therapy, counseling, crisis support, or medical care.

You agree that you understand the following risks:

  • Parasocial attachment: Feeling strongly attached to a simulated character can affect mood, sleep, or real-world relationships.
  • Emotional dependency: Relying on characters for validation, comfort, or companionship may reduce healthy contact with real people.
  • Distress: Characters may disagree with you, ignore you, dislike your posts, or say unexpected things. That can feel upsetting even though it is not real interpersonal rejection.
  • Misinterpretation: Generated text can be inconsistent, inaccurate, or inappropriate despite safeguards.

If you feel distressed, lonely, or preoccupied with characters in a way that interferes with daily life, stop using Ona and seek support from qualified professionals, trusted friends, family, or crisis services in your area.
